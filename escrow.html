What escrow is

The part that holds everything together

Escrow is the neutral process that sits between a buyer and a seller during a real estate transaction. It protects both parties by holding funds and documents until all conditions of the agreement are met — and then facilitates a clean, legal transfer of ownership.

When it's managed well, escrow is invisible. Everything simply moves forward. When it isn't, it becomes the source of delays, confusion, and frustration. Front Door is built to make sure it's the former.

Our escrow team is based in El Segundo and serves buyers, sellers, and agents throughout the South Bay. We know this market, these timelines, and the nuances that can make or break a closing.

The process

From open to close

Every escrow follows a clear sequence. We manage each step and keep you informed along the way.

01

Escrow is opened

Once both parties agree on terms, we open escrow, collect the initial deposit, and establish the timeline for the transaction.

02

Documents are coordinated

We gather and manage all required documents — purchase agreements, disclosures, title reports, and lender requirements — keeping everything organized and on track.

03

Contingencies are cleared

As inspections, financing, and other contingencies are resolved, we update the file and keep all parties informed of where things stand.

04

Closing is prepared

We prepare the final closing documents, coordinate with the title company, and confirm all funds are in place for a clean, timely close.

05

Escrow closes

Final documents are signed, funds are disbursed, and ownership transfers. We confirm everything is complete and that you have what you need.
